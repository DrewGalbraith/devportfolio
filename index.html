About Me

My name is Drew Galbraith. I study large language models (LLMs). As an undergrad ,I studied linguistics, fascinated by concepts life the Sapir-Whorf Hypothesis, language prestige and stigma shape our society so strongly, and even mapping linguistic events in brain activity with EEG. All that lead me to natural language processing (NLP) where I discovered I had a knack for manipulating text and for programming generally. I began investing a lot into my technical skills, including joining a univesity lab to research LLMs. Shortly thereafter, ChatGPT was released and public interest in the field went through the roof. Suddenly, I didn't have to rely on odd analogies to explain what I work on. I have been enjoying this field everyday since.

Ultimately, I want to use LLMs (and ML technologies generally) to lift the lives of those least advantaged by technology. Dr. David Wingate aptly said that "these technologies take [my life and your life] from an 8 to an 8.7; but they could also take the life of someone in poverty, someone lacking a formal education, or someone who doesn't have code documentation in their language, etc. from a 3 to a 7." That's the change I see and the technology I want to work toward. A key point of approach is contributing to open research, open code, and open model artifacts. Open source, as PyTorch founder Soumith Chintala described, is the best way to level the playing field (he's one of my tech heroes).

In my free time, I like paddle sports, reading from my book list, and playing guitar. I also enjoy trying to solve games I like playing with my growing knowledge of algorithms. Doing so tends to ruin the games for me. Check out my blog to read about some of my projects and feel free to reach out!
